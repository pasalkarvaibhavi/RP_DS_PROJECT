# 🌟 **What Does mL/kg/day Mean? (Very Simple Explanation)**

This unit is used in NICUs (Neonatal Intensive Care Units) to decide **how much milk a baby can safely drink in one day**.

Break it into pieces:

*   **mL** = milliliters (amount of milk)
*   **kg** = baby’s weight in kilograms
*   **per day** = in 24 hours

So:

# 👉 **mL/kg/day = how many milliliters of milk a baby can drink per kilogram of body weight in a day**

***

# 🍼 **Why do NICUs use mL/kg/day?**

Because **premature babies are very small**, often 0.8–1.2 kg.

Instead of giving all babies the same amount, doctors calculate milk based on:

> **Baby size (weight)**

This ensures safety and prevents:

*   Overfeeding
*   Fluid overload
*   Kidney strain
*   Heart stress

Your research paper uses a standard feeding volume limit:

📌 **NICU safe limit = 160 mL/kg/day** [\[nin.res.in\]](https://nin.res.in/dietaryguidelines/pdfjs/locale/DGI_2024.pdf)

***

# 🌟 **Simple Example**

### If a baby weighs:

**1.0 kg**

and the NICU limit is:

**160 mL/kg/day**

Then:

    1.0 kg × 160 mL = 160 mL per day

That’s all the milk the baby can safely take.

***

# ⭐ Example 2: Baby weighs 1.4 kg

    1.4 × 160 = 224 mL/day

So this baby can safely drink **224 mL/day**.

***

# 🌟 **Why is this important for your PHASE 6 calculations?**

Because your system calculates:

*   how much protein baby gets
*   how many calories baby gets

**based on how much milk the baby is allowed to drink per day**.

If milk is weak, the baby might need:

*   more than **160 mL/kg/day** to meet nutrition targets

BUT this is **not allowed** because of safety limits.

So if your system calculates:

    Baby needs: 190 mL/kg/day
    Limit: 160 mL/kg/day

👉 Fortifier is required

# 🌟 **Where does the 160 mL/kg/day value come from?**

From neonatal nutrition research:

> NICUs use **160 ml/kg/day** as the safe upper limit for premature infants to prevent fluid overload.    [\[nin.res.in\]](https://nin.res.in/dietaryguidelines/pdfjs/locale/DGI_2024.pdf)

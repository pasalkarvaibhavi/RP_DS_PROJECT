# ✅ **8.1 Functional Testing (Does the app actually work?)**

You test:

### ✔ Manual input mode

Enter known values and verify:

*   Calories are correct
*   Protein delivered is correct
*   Fortifier recommendation matches medical logic

### ✔ AI prediction mode

Enter:

*   Age of milk
*   Term category
*   Days between pumps
*   Pump dates
*   Lactation stage

Check:

*   AI predicts fat/protein/lactose
*   Nutrition logic runs
*   Results make sense

***

# 🧪 Example Test Case (you can include this in your report)

**Input:**  
Fat = 4.0  
Protein = 1.2  
Lactose = 7.3  
Baby weight = 1.4 kg  
Volume limit = 160  
Protein target = 3.5  
Calorie target = 110

**Output Expected:**

*   Calories ≈ 20.4 kcal/dL
*   Protein delivered ≈ 1.92 g/kg/day
*   Not meeting protein target
*   Fortifier: HIGH

Use this in your testing table.

***

# ✅ **8.2 Extreme Boundary Testing**

Test:

*   Fat = 0.5
*   Protein = 0.5
*   Lactose = 5.0

App should still calculate results, but show milk is too weak.

Test:

*   Very strong milk (fat = 8, protein = 2)  
    App should say NO fortifier needed.

***

# ✅ **8.3 User Interface Testing**

Check:

*   All fields visible
*   Medical information expanders work
*   Buttons respond correctly
*   Errors handled (like blank inputs)

***

# ✅ **8.4 Model Validation**

Verify your Phase 5 models by checking:

*   R² score

*   Predicted vs actual scatter plots

# ✅ **8.5 Clinical Decision Validation**

Check if your logic matches real NICU rules:

*   Does it warn when volume > 160 ml/kg/day?
*   Does it recommend stronger fortifier when protein is lower?
*   Does it correctly compute calorie delivery?

You can validate with examples from the research paper itself.  
(See your Nature source: macronutrient-driven calorie differences and adequacy)    [\[nin.res.in\]](https://nin.res.in/dietaryguidelines/pdfjs/locale/DGI_2024.pdf)
